Editor's note: Consider reading the [thesis paper](/documents/Luka_Mihailovic_diplomski_rad.pdf) about this piece of software. Translation from Serbian to other languages is left as an exercise for the reader.

This started as an idea for a personal project, since I had previously tortured
myself with handwritten HTML, and the slog of manually copying and pasting headers,
footers and the likes of.

It wasn't long before I discovered _static site generators_. And none of them fit my needs.

One half was written in JavaScript (_shudders_), the other half was [Hugo](https://gohugo.io/).

I had used Hugo for a certain period of time, working professionally with it, and there I learned enough about it to know that there is too much of it for my personal needs. It really is a serious piece of software, too serious for me, and I craved something smaller, with **much** less configuration.

Then, inspiration struck, and I realised I could use this as the subject of my degree's thesis! I'd write my own solution, and use my Go knowledge to flex on the rest of my peers and faculty staff.

Bragging rights and a functional piece of software tuned just to my needs sounded too appealing, and the rest was history (as written down in great detail with commits on the `master` branch), as they say.
